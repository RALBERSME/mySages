Summary: From the "Seven Sages" of ancient times, we can learn fundamental life lessons, including the importance of moderation, self-knowledge, the continuous pursuit of knowledge, social responsibility, and the acceptance of uncertainty. These principles are timeless and help us lead balanced, reflective and fulfilling lives.
Examples of teachings from the Seven Sages:

"Nothing in excess!"
(Attributed to Democritus, but often associated with the Seven Sages) – This maxim emphasizes the importance of moderation in all areas of life in order to find balance and well-being.
"Know thyself!"
– This is a call for self-knowledge and reflection in order to understand one's own strengths, weaknesses, and motivations.
"Everything is practice!"
This teaching emphasizes that mastery in skills or in life is only achieved through constant practice and striving for improvement.
Strive for knowledge and increase knowledge (Thales of Miletus)
This principle encourages us to remain curious, to seek knowledge and never to be satisfied with the current state of knowledge.
These teachings are timeless, universal, and still relevant today. They offer a holistic approach to a successful and happy life that goes beyond mere intelligence.
